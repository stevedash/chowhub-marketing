Switching from Clover

No more $100/mo per device.

Clover is Fiserv-owned and sold mostly through banks and ISOs, which is why pricing varies wildly. Common reality: $100–$180/mo per device on the Hospitality tier, plus add-on apps stacking $10–$50/mo each, plus the Fiserv processing lock-in.

Where ChowHub wins

Math.

  • Roughly 1/3 to 1/2 the monthly cost at the same feature level
  • No per-device monthly tax — adding a station is hardware-only
  • No 3-year contract via a bank sales rep
  • No Clover hardware purchase — your Pi or laptop works fine
  • Recipe inventory included, not a paid app
  • Self-hostable for venues that prefer it
Where Clover wins

Polish and ecosystem.

  • Massive third-party app marketplace — loyalty, scheduling, payroll, accounting
  • Polished, purpose-built first-party hardware
  • Built-in customer-facing display on Mini / Station
  • Well-known brand customers recognize at the counter
  • Native gift card physical-card fulfillment

Best fit for ChowHub

Anyone running 2+ Clover devices and watching the monthly bill climb. The per-device economics flip dramatically once you stop paying $100+/mo per terminal.

Stay on Clover if…

You depend on a specific Clover marketplace app (loyalty integrations, physical gift cards, niche payroll) and the bank relationship that comes with the deal works for you.

Migration in plain English

It's a one-evening switch.

Export your existing menu to CSV. Import it into ChowHub with the bulk loader. Connect your Stripe or Square account (you can keep the one you already use). Pick a slow weekday — Tuesday afternoon works for most — and switch over. Run both systems in parallel for the first lunch if it helps you sleep. We can answer migration questions on email at any point.
